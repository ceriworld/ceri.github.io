CERI Launches Country & Regions Section on Official Website, Beginning with France, Colombia, and the United States
Press release • 20 April 2026

Summary: New digital platform feature reflects CERI's commitment to transparency, data access, and intergovernmental cooperation

PARIS, FRANCE — The Centre for Economic Research and Intergovernmental Cooperation (CERI) today announced the launch of a new Countries & Regions section on its official website, marking a significant step in making key economic data and national indicators publicly accessible through a single, unified platform.

The initial release features dedicated pages for three countries — France, Colombia, and the United States — each presenting curated economic data and indicators relevant to policymakers, researchers, partner institutions, and the general public. These three nations represent the founding member states of CERI's ongoing intergovernmental framework, making their inclusion in this inaugural launch particularly meaningful.

This development aligns with CERI's core mandate across the areas of economics, education, health, and the promotion of world peace — ensuring that reliable, structured information is available to all stakeholders engaged in international dialogue and cooperation.

"Access to clear and credible economic data is foundational to informed decision-making at every level of governance," a CERI representative stated. "This platform feature is a direct expression of our institutional commitment to openness and to equipping member states and partner nations with the tools they need."

CERI anticipates expanding the Countries & Regions section to include additional nations as its intergovernmental reach grows. The organization is currently advancing efforts to formalize its status as an intergovernmental organization (IGO), with an initial treaty framework anchored in France, Colombia, and the United States.
